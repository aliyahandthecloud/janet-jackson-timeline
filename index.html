Janet Jackson

ICON • LEGEND • INNOVATOR

1966

Janet Damita Jo Jackson was born on May 16, 1966, in Gary, Indiana. She was the youngest of ten children in the legendary Jackson family, born into a household that would change music history forever.

1970s

Growing up in the spotlight, Janet appeared on variety shows with her famous brothers. She made her stage debut at age seven at the MGM Grand in Las Vegas, beginning her journey in entertainment.

1977

Janet began her acting career, appearing in shows like "Good Times" and "Diff'rent Strokes." She later starred in "Fame" as Cleo Hewitt, showcasing her versatility as a performer.

1982

At just 16, Janet released her self-titled debut album. While modest in commercial success, it marked the beginning of her musical journey and laid the foundation for what was to come.

1986

"Control" changed everything. Teaming with Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis, Janet declared her independence and established herself as a force in pop music. The album sold over 10 million copies worldwide.

1989

"Rhythm Nation 1814" became a cultural phenomenon, addressing social issues through music. Janet made history as the first artist to achieve seven top-five hits from a single album.

1993

With "janet." she explored sensuality and intimacy, creating one of the best-selling albums of the decade. The album debuted at #1 and solidified her status as a global superstar.

1997

"The Velvet Rope" showcased Janet's vulnerability, addressing depression, self-worth, and social issues. Critics hailed it as her most personal and mature work, earning widespread critical acclaim.

2000s

Janet continued to dominate with "All for You" (2001), which debuted at #1 and earned a Grammy. She released "Damita Jo" (2004) and "20 Y.O." (2006), continuing to push creative boundaries while navigating industry challenges with grace and resilience.

2010s

After a seven-year hiatus, Janet returned triumphantly with "Unbreakable" (2015), debuting at #1 on the Billboard 200. She embarked on the State of the World Tour and became a mother, balancing her personal life with her legendary career.

Present

Janet Jackson continues to inspire and innovate. Inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame (2019), she remains a cultural icon and influence on artists worldwide. Her legacy as a pioneer in music, dance, and social activism is firmly cemented, and her impact continues to resonate across generations.
